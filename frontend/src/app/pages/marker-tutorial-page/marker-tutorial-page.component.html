Choose category
Choose category Step 1

First, choose category of the data you want to label. After choosing category you will be taken to marker page.
Find anomaly
Find anomaly Step 2

Use the slider on your right to search for anomalies on the picture. Remember that by default you are given only a part of the scan. To download rest of the images, use the slider to go to the end of available view.

If you haven't found any anomalies on the scan, click 'Skip scan' button to draw another set of images.
Label it
Label it Step 3

Label part of the image that you find interesting. Using the slider proceed to next levels of the scan to label whole anomaly. Remember that you can always delete your label and create it again.

With every annotation you are building a 3D label that will be defined by drawn surfaces.

In this example we are showing how to label fragments of patient ribs.
Send label
Send label Step 4

After creating label, click 'Send label' button. You still have option of further labeling the scan after clicking this button.

After sending all of the labels, please click 'Next scan' button to draw another scan for labelling.
